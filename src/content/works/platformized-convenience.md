## Abstract-level note

The paper treats convenience as a social arrangement rather than a consumer feeling. Platforms ease search, payment, coordination, and discovery for users while relocating temporal, organizational, and exit-related burdens elsewhere in the chain.

It follows this through service coordination, discovery coordination, and integrated coordination, asking how repeated passage through fewer interfaces changes the ordinary routes by which life becomes manageable.
