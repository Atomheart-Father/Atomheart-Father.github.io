## Abstract-level note

This paper argues that contemporary platform power can no longer be described only as a politics of visibility. A growing share of first-pass interpretation is now assembled inside answer-first interfaces that return a provisional reading before longer source inspection begins.

The argument is organized around a six-part mechanism linking dispersed production to recursive closure: machine-readable compression, attention orchestration, interpretive compression, subject adaptation, and a feedback loop through which default answers become easier to reissue.
