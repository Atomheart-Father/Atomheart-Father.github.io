## Abstract-level note

This paper approaches contemporary AI as a governance problem for surveillance societies. As systems move from bounded models toward tool use, permissions, and modular skill ecosystems, the boundary of what a system can do becomes harder to stabilize.

The paper develops two linked diagnoses: capability boundary drift and audit boundary collapse. Under these conditions, transparency risks becoming performative rather than contestable.
