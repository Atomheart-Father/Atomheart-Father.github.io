## Project frame

This work studies a specific risk in AI-mediated knowledge systems: centralized interpretive pipelines may do more than retrieve and summarize information. They can privilege canonical sources, compress conflict, and route novelty back into default frames.

The current manuscript tests this through a minimal auditable pipeline spanning corpus construction, retrieval, ranking, structured summary, explanation, and limited intervention checks.

## Current empirical anchor

The active validation anchor is automated hiring and candidate review. The scope is intentionally narrow: job advertising, candidate intake, resume screening, ranking or scoring, interview support, and candidate evaluation memos.

The public summary does not expose internal study labels, assignment metadata, participant lists, or raw responses.

## Questionnaire status

The participant-facing questionnaire package is prepared in Chinese and English. It is currently designed for manual distribution, not open public download.

For now, the website can host the project description and questionnaire status. If the study later moves to a web questionnaire, the page can point to an external form system or a static questionnaire interface without changing the research project structure.
